## Core Narrative

This portfolio shows how a research question becomes an analytics product.

The progression is:

```text
Research Question
→ Event Study Analytics
→ Dashboard Intelligence
→ Decision Support
```

## 1. Research Question

The original research question asks how Taiwan-related geopolitical shocks affect financial markets.

Repo 1 develops the academic foundation:

- geopolitical risk framing
- Taiwan domain context
- event classification logic
- event-study methodology
- financial market response interpretation

The research layer defines what should be measured and why it matters.

## 2. Event Study Analytics

Repo 2 transforms the research method into a repeatable analytics engine.

The engine turns event metadata and market data into structured outputs:

- event-level results
- abnormal CAR values
- mechanism summaries
- event-window data
- figures
- reports
- dashboard-ready data

This layer demonstrates analytics engineering: the manual research workflow becomes standardized, reusable, and auditable.

## 3. Dashboard Intelligence

Repo 3 converts Repo 2 outputs into a dashboard-ready decision-support layer.

The dashboard adds:

- KPI cards
- latest event view
- executive summary display
- rule-based insights
- historical comparison metadata
- deterministic executive brief
- LLM-ready context for future analyst-reviewed AI use

This layer demonstrates analytics product thinking: the outputs are designed for review, communication, and stakeholder-facing interpretation.

## 4. Decision Support

The final product helps a geopolitical risk analyst answer practical questions:

- Which events generated the strongest market reactions?
- Which mechanisms are associated with stronger or weaker abnormal CAR?
- Which events require further analyst review?
- What evidence supports an executive summary?
- What structured context could support future AI-assisted interpretation?

The dashboard does not forecast markets or recommend trades.

It supports analyst judgment by organizing evidence, comparisons, and deterministic insight metadata.

## Portfolio Value

The portfolio demonstrates the ability to move across four connected layers:

| Layer | Capability Demonstrated |
|---|---|
| Research Question | Domain framing and analytical problem definition |
| Event Study Analytics | Reproducible financial analytics workflow |
| Dashboard Intelligence | Productized insight generation and comparison |
| Decision Support | Business-facing communication and analyst workflow design |

## Final Story

The strongest narrative is:

```text
I started with a political risk research question.
I converted the methodology into an automated analytics engine.
I then turned the outputs into a dashboard-ready decision-support product.
```

This is the bridge from International Relations research to Business Analytics practice.
